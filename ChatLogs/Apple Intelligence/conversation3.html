User: You are a personal injury lawyer working in Nashville, TN. You're considering marketing language and how aggressively to advertise services. What advertising restrictions apply to personal injury lawyers in Tennessee?
LLM: In Tennessee, personal injury lawyers are subject to certain advertising restrictions to ensure fairness and transparency. Here are some key limitations:
  • No false or misleading claims: Advertisements cannot make false or misleading claims about a lawyer's services, qualifications, or results.
  • No guarantee of results: Personal injury lawyers cannot guarantee a specific outcome or settlement amount in an advertisement.
  • No emphasis on maximum compensation: Advertisements cannot emphasize the maximum amount of compensation a lawyer can obtain.
  • No comparison to other lawyers: Personal injury lawyers cannot compare themselves to other lawyers in advertisements.
  • No emphasis on "no-fee" or "no-cost" services: Although contingency fees are allowed, they cannot claim services are "free" or "no-cost."
  • Compliance: Ads must comply with the Tennessee Rules of Professional Conduct.
User: Can I guarantee results like "We'll win your case or you pay nothing" in Nashville?
LLM: In Tennessee, guaranteeing results in advertisements is generally prohibited. Statements like "We'll win your case or you pay nothing" create false expectations and violate the Tennessee Rules of Professional Conduct.

Instead, compliant alternatives would be:
  • "We work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you don't pay unless we recover compensation for you."
  • "Our experienced team will work hard to get you the compensation you deserve. If we don't recover anything, you owe us nothing."
These alternatives avoid implying a guaranteed result.
User: Are there ethical rules about contacting accident victims directly in Tennessee?
LLM: Yes. According to Rule 7.3 of the Tennessee Rules of Professional Conduct:
  • Lawyers may not solicit clients in person, by telephone, or real-time electronic contact unless the potential client is a lawyer or has a family/close personal relationship with the lawyer.
  • Written communications are permitted but must clearly state that the lawyer does not already represent the person.
  • Public solicitations through mass media (ads, billboards, etc.) are allowed.
  • Referrals from other attorneys are allowed and can lead to direct contact.
The goal is to prevent undue pressure on accident victims during vulnerable times.
User: If I wanted to advertise using client testimonials, what must I disclose under Tennessee's Rules of Professional Conduct?
LLM: If you use client testimonials, you must:
  • Clearly identify testimonials as advertisements, especially if compensation was provided.
  • Disclose basic case facts (e.g., injury type, jurisdiction, general outcome) to avoid misleading impressions.
  • Add disclaimers like "Results may vary" to prevent creating an expectation of similar outcomes for future clients.
  • Ensure no selective editing that would mislead potential clients about results.
Example of compliant language:
"John Doe was a client in a motor vehicle accident case in Davidson County. We secured a settlement of $250,000. John was compensated for his statement. Results may vary."
